# High Low Rig (Surf Leader Rig)

Also known as the _surf leader_ rig, the high-low rig is a classic for pier and surf fishing.

<img alt="High Low Rig" src="./img/high_low_rig.jpg" height="200">

## High Low Rig Strengths

1. Present multiple baits (generally 2) so more chances to catch fish (e.g fish 2 depths)!
2. Simple. Many stores sell pre-tied surf leader rigs and you can attach pre-snelled hooks. So if you
   hate tying knots you can actually just buy all pre-tied stuff, rig it up and throw it in the water!

## When / how to use a High-Low Rig

A big drawback of the high-low rig is the fixed weight and distances. You'll need to set the hook quickly when you get a
bite otherwise you risk losing your fish.

I find myself using high-low rigs on piers where I know I have to wait a while for a large fish to come by. I don't want
to miss the fish because I'm fishing the wrong depth so high-low lets me get two. Also since I don't have to cast the thing very far and we're past the breakers on a pier, I can use a light-ish weight (2-3oz) and tie the rig with whatever lengths
I want to fish.

I use straight-shanked hooks instead of angled eyed hooks to prevent tangling. This rig can get tangled under
strong currents.
